News + Chat + LMS Mobile App

A comprehensive "Super App" integrating news feeds, real-time chat, and learning modules.

Problem

Users previously had to switch between three different apps to stay updated, communicate with peers, and access course material. This scattered experience caused friction and significant user drop-offs. The client wanted a "Super App" combining these distinct flows.

Research

Information architecture was the primary challenge. I conducted extensive card sorting exercises with 20 initial users. The data showed that users wanted "Chats" and "News" to be instantly accessible upon opening the app, while "LMS Modules" could be secondary since they were accessed during dedicated study sessions.

User Flow

Home Screen (News Feed) → Tap on Article to read → Share article via internal Chat overlay → Navigate to associated LMS Module explicitly linked in Article context → Return to global navigation seamlessly.

Wireframes

The wireframes centered around a persistent bottom navigation bar. I tested three different navigational patterns before landing on a 5-icon structure (Home/News, Courses, Action Button, Chats, Profile) that users found most familiar.

Final UI

The final interface features a clean, white-space heavy layout to offset the high cognitive load of consuming varied content. The UI includes a dark mode optimization to reduce eye strain during long chat or reading sessions.
