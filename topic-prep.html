TopicPrep Mobile App

A gamified mobile learning app designed to boost student engagement and retention.

The Problem

In the competitive landscape of standardized testing, students often face 'study fatigue'—a state where traditional textbooks and rote memorization lead to diminishing returns. The core challenge was not just to digitize the curriculum, but to transform the preparation process into a compelling habit. We needed a solution that would make 20 minutes of study feel like 5 minutes of play, without compromising on academic rigor.

My Role & Process

As the Lead Product Designer, I shepherded this project from a vague concept to a polished MVP.

  • Discovery: Conducted 15+ user interviews with high school students to identify pain points in current study habits.
  • Ideation: Created low-fidelity wireframes in Figma, focusing on 'micro-interactions' that reward progress.
  • Validation: Ran A/B tests on two distinct navigation patterns. The 'Journey Map' style outperformed the traditional list view by 25% in usability tests.
  • Handoff: Delivered a comprehensive design system to the development team, ensuring pixel-perfect implementation.

The Outcome

The resulting prototype received a System Usability Scale (SUS) score of 85/100 during beta testing, placing it in the top percentile of educational apps. Early metrics from the beta launch show a 40% increase in average session duration compared to competitors. The app is now in the final stages of QA and is scheduled for a wide release on the Google Play Store next month.
